The Rule of 3

Seminar 3 · Tutorial
When the chat stays digital for too long, it's usually not destiny.

T

here comes a moment in every modern courtship where you must apply the oldest filter in the book — dressed up for 2026 as The Rule of 3.

If by the end of three calendar days of consistent texting you do not have a concrete date on the books (time, place, rough plan), move along.

“She's not 'busy.' She's not 'figuring things out.' She's just not that into you — and she's enjoying the attention while it lasts.”

Core Principle

A grown man does not have time to play endless pen-pal with someone who's only bored, lonely, or collecting orbiters. Texting marathons without escalation = entertainment for her, time-sink for you.

Three days. No date secured? Next.

Diagnostic Traffic Lights

Green Light – Proceed
She suggests times/locations herself or locks in your proposal quickly.
She asks questions about you beyond surface level.
Energy is reciprocal — she matches or exceeds your effort.
Yellow Light – Caution
Replies are consistent but short/vague (“haha yeah”, “maybe”, “idk”).
She keeps the chat alive but dodges date logistics.
You're the one always restarting the conversation.
Give 24 more hours max — then force the issue or walk.
Red Light – Eject
Three full days pass with texting but zero date set.
Dry responses, ghosting streaks, or “I'm just not good at texting” excuses.
You're carrying 80–100% of the conversation weight.
She's bored-texting. Your time is finite. Protect it.

Product of a female-dominated household. Reluctant field researcher.
